"Extreme Weather: Flooding"

Hotter air can hold more water droplets, and a warmer surface temperature condenses more water. The result is more rain. According to the IPCC report from 2018, climate change has probably not influenced the magnitude or frequency of floods, but it has a “detectable influence” on parts of the hydrological cycle like precipitation and snowmelt, which in term influence floods.
