Common false statements

"The climate has always changed"

The climate has always changed, but not on a global scale at the same time. Only the last 150 years show a near global-coherence in the rapid global warming, while previous centuries lack spatio-temporal coherence.

See more details and quotation

"It's just the sun"

The changes in temperature over the last decades have been too significant and too rapid as to be linked to solar activity or changes in earth's orbit. Also, solar energy received by earth has been decreasing in the last couple of centuries. Find out more

Go to graphic

"There is no scientific consensus on climate change"

Almost every paper with a position on climate change (97%) supports the overall consensus of human-made global climate change. Furthermore, above 99.9 percent of scientists publishing today also accept that position.

See more details and quotation

"Climate models are unreliable & don’t match observations"

The projections of a big majority of past climate change models (14 out of 17) match the measured GSTM (global mean surface temperature) increase (see quotation). In addition to that, all models are being ‘hindcasted’. Meaning: Tested with known data from the past to measure the accuracy of its predictions.

See more details and quotation

"Climate Change isn’t human-made"

Only human-made greenhouse gas emissions can explain the current change in the climate. Natural causes alone would have instead led to a cooling of the planet.

See quotation
